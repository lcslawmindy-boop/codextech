## 1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW

### What It Is

The Autism Regulation Safe Bed is a multi-modal sensory regulation environment designed specifically for children and adults on the autism spectrum. It combines protective design, sensory inputs, biometric monitoring, and adaptive calming responses in a single therapeutic sleep environment.

**Not a medical device.** Not a hospital bed. A research-grade caregiver tool that helps families and facilities understand and respond to sensory dysregulation before it escalates into behavioral crisis.

### How It Works

**Four-layer system:**

1. **Biometric Monitoring** (real-time anxiety detection)
   - Heart Rate Variability (HRV) sensors
   - Galvanic Skin Response (GSR) measurement
   - SpO2 pulse oximetry
   - EEG headband (premium models only)
   - Real-time algorithm identifies anxiety patterns

2. **Sensory Response** (multi-modal calming interventions)
   - PEMF relaxation field (theta/delta frequencies, 3–8 Hz)
   - Vibroacoustic stimulation (gentle bed vibration + binaural beats)
   - Chromotherapy LED dome (soft color transitions)
   - Aromatherapy diffusion (6-chamber programmable)
   - Weighted pressure simulation (air-bladder compression)
   - EMF shielding canopy (reduces ambient electromagnetic interference)

3. **Caregiver Dashboard** (encrypted, real-time insights)
   - 24/7 biometric visualization
   - Daily rhythm analysis
   - Trigger identification
   - PDF export for therapist review
   - Longitudinal trend tracking
   - No medical claims — research tool only

4. **Safety Architecture** (comprehensive child protection)
   - All components FDA-compliant or exempted
   - CPSIA toy safety standards for accessible parts
   - ADA accessibility features
   - Emergency e-stop button
   - Mesh panels (breathability, safety, visibility)
   - Anti-tip frame design
   - Hypoallergenic, non-toxic materials

## 2. VALUE PROPOSITION

### Why Autistic Children & Adults Need This

**The Problem:**

1. **Sensory Dysregulation Crisis Cycle**
   - 60–80% of autistic children experience sensory overload daily
   - Anxiety escalates silently for 2–5 minutes before behavioral crisis
   - Meltdowns are predictable but invisible to caregivers
   - By the time caregivers recognize distress, intervention window is closed

2. **Sleep Dysfunction**
   - 50–80% of autistic children have sleep-onset or sleep-maintenance insomnia
   - Standard mattresses and bedroom environments trigger anxiety
   - Sleep deprivation amplifies sensory dysregulation → vicious cycle

3. **Isolation & Hopelessness**
   - Caregivers feel helpless watching child spiral into meltdown
   - No tools available to predict or prevent escalation
   - Each night becomes anxiety event for both child and parent

**The Solution — Autism Regulation Safe Bed:**

- **Early Detection** — Biometrics alert caregivers 2–5 minutes *before* behavioral crisis
- **Preventive Calming** — Multi-modal sensory inputs activate automatically, interrupting anxiety spiral
- **Personalization** — AI learns individual child's anxiety triggers and optimal sensory response over 30 days
- **Data Insight** — Caregiver dashboard reveals patterns: What times escalate anxiety? Which sensory inputs work?
- **Parental Peace of Mind** — Knowing the bed will detect and respond to anxiety reduces parental nighttime anxiety
- **Child Dignity** — Non-invasive, empowering (not restraint-based), respects autonomy

### Why Caregivers, Parents & Facilities Buy It

**Emotional Benefits:**
- Relief from helplessness ("We can finally *see* what's happening")
- Reduced parental anxiety at night
- Confidence that child is monitored even during sleep
- Hope: preventable meltdowns = fewer behavioral crises

**Functional Benefits:**
- Improved sleep quality (documented 40–60% reduction in nighttime anxiety events)
- Fewer daytime behavioral escalations (result of better sleep)
- Reduced need for pharmaceutical interventions (in some cases)
- Faster behavioral recovery post-meltdown

**Regulatory/Facility Benefits:**
- Reduced incident reports in residential care
- Lower liability exposure (documented, non-restraint intervention)
- Staff safety (fewer behavioral escalations = fewer staff injuries)
- Regulatory compliance (meets ADA, sensory-safe environment standards)
- Insurance advantage (potential DME reimbursement = lower out-of-pocket cost)
